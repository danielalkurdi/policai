We're excited to announce the first official release of Policai — an open-source tracker for Australian AI policy and governance developments.

## What is Policai?

Policai aggregates, analyses, and visualises AI policy developments across all Australian jurisdictions — federal, state, and territory. It covers legislation, regulation, guidelines, frameworks, and standards related to artificial intelligence, all in one searchable interface.

## What's in v0.1.0

This release includes the core platform with several key features:

### Policy Browser

A searchable, filterable list of Australian AI policies. Filter by jurisdiction, policy type, or status. Each policy has a detail page with an AI-generated summary, related policies, and source links.

### Interactive Map

An interactive map of Australia showing policy activity by state and territory. Click any jurisdiction to see its active policies.

### Autonomous Scraper

Policai monitors 8 Australian government sources for new AI policy content. The scraper runs automatically, sends content through AI analysis for relevance scoring, and either publishes high-confidence findings or queues them for review.

### Dark Mode

Full system-level dark mode support across the entire application.

### This Blog

And of course, this blog — where we'll share project updates, new data source announcements, and commentary on significant AI policy developments.

## Open Source

Policai is fully open source. Check out the repository on GitHub to explore the code, report issues, or contribute.

## What's Next

We're continuing to expand coverage of Australian AI policy sources, improve the AI analysis pipeline, and add new visualisation features. Stay tuned for updates here on the blog.
